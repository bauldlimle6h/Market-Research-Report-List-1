<p><p>The prefilled softgel capsule market is segmented into two main types: gelatin-based and non-animal-based capsules. Gelatin softgels are derived from animal sources, primarily bovine or porcine, and are widely used due to their cost-effectiveness and ability to encapsulate a variety of formulations. Conversely, non-animal softgels, often made from plant-derived materials like hydroxypropyl methylcellulose (HPMC), cater to vegetarian and vegan consumers, addressing dietary restrictions and ethical concerns. Both types serve diverse applications in pharmaceuticals and nutraceuticals.</p></p>
