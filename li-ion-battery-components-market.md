<p><p>The Li-ion battery components market is segmented into four main types: cathodes, anodes, electrolytes, and separators. Cathodes, typically made from materials like lithium cobalt oxide, are crucial for energy storage. Anodes, usually composed of graphite, enable lithium ion movement during charging. Electrolytes, which can be liquid, gel, or solid, facilitate ion transfer between cathode and anode. Separators, often made of porous materials, prevent direct contact between electrodes, ensuring safety and efficiency. Each component plays a vital role in overall battery performance.</p></p>
