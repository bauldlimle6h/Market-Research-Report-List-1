<p><ul><li>Dry Fermentation</li><li>Wet Fermentation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biodigester market is primarily divided into two types: dry fermentation and wet fermentation. Dry fermentation involves the anaerobic digestion of high-solids organic materials, often used for agricultural waste, and results in biogas production with lower water content. In contrast, wet fermentation utilizes low-solids feedstocks, such as wastewater or food scraps, requiring more water and offering higher biogas yields. Both methods contribute to sustainable waste management and renewable energy generation, catering to different waste characteristics and processing needs.</p></p>
