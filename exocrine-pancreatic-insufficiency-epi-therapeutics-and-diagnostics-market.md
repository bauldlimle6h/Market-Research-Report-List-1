<p><p>Exocrine Pancreatic Insufficiency (EPI) is a condition characterized by the inadequate production of digestive enzymes by the pancreas, leading to malabsorption and nutritional deficiencies. The market for EPI therapeutics and diagnostics is expanding, driven by rising prevalence of conditions like pancreatitis, cystic fibrosis, and diabetes that contribute to EPI. Current therapeutic options primarily include pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT) and supportive nutritional therapies. Additionally, diagnostic methodologies such as stool tests, breath tests, and imaging techniques are advancing, enhancing the detection rates of EPI.</p><p>The market growth can be attributed to increasing awareness of EPI, improvements in diagnostic technologies, and a growing emphasis on early intervention strategies. Innovations in enzyme formulations and the development of more effective and patient-friendly delivery systems are also shaping the landscape of EPI treatment.
