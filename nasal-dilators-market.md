<p><p>The nasal dilators market is categorized into two main types: external and internal. External nasal dilators are adhesive strips applied to the outside of the nose, widening the nostrils to enhance airflow, often used during exercise or sleep. Internal nasal dilators, on the other hand, are devices inserted inside the nostrils to maintain open passages and alleviate breathing difficulties, particularly for those with nasal obstructions. Both types cater to individuals seeking improved respiratory function and comfort.</p></p>
