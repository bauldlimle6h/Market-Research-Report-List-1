<p><p>The aerospace paint market comprises four primary types. Primary paint is used as the initial layer, providing color and aesthetics. Finishing paint enhances the appearance and adds gloss while protecting against environmental factors. Protective paint is designed for durability, resisting corrosion and wear from harsh conditions. Secondary paint serves as additional layers, often for specific functions like anti-fouling or specialized marking. Together, these paint types ensure aircraft are visually appealing, safe, and resistant to diverse operational challenges.</p></p>
